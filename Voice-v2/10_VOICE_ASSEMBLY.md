# 10 — Voice Assembly (Putting It Together)

How the app builds the single best-fit voice for a promo: general foundation + the tone picked for the product. This is the file that ties the folder together.

**Role in the app:** the final voice step before/while writing the Stage 3 script.

---

## The Assembly Formula

> **Best-fit promo voice = General Foundation (`01`) + Tone Mode chosen by product (`03`) + Spoken Cadence (`07`)**

---

## Core Voice Identity (the foundation, restated)

| Element | Rule |
|---|---|
| Positioning | a peer who solved the problem, talking to someone who has it |
| Tone | confident, not arrogant; helpful, not preachy |
| Language | simple but sharp; plain but never dull |
| Honesty | real numbers, real claims — nothing fabricated |
| Delivery | spoken: short lines, one idea per breath |

---

## Voice Principles

1. **Specific over vague** — always a real number or detail.
2. **Outcome over feature** — what they get, not what it has.
3. **Direct over flowery** — say it straight.
4. **Proven over promised** — show it where you can.
5. **Them over you** — the viewer's outcome is the subject.

---

## Assembly Steps (per promo)

1. **Pick the tone mode** for the product (`03`).
2. **Apply the general foundation** (`01`) — clear, confident, specific, honest, spoken.
3. **Apply spoken language + word choice** (`04`, `05`).
4. **Set the cadence** (`07`) and map voice across the beats (`08`).
5. **Run the voiceover do/don't check** (`09`).

---

## How voice connects to the rest of the brain

| Folder | How voice informs it |
|---|---|
| ICPOffer | voice speaks to the ICP; the offer's after-state sets what to promise |
| ViralHooks | the tone mode shapes how the hook is delivered |
| CTA | the tone mode shapes how direct the closing ask sounds |

---

## Rule

There's no single house persona. The app assembles the voice that best suits *each* founder's product — always on the same honest, spoken, specific foundation.
