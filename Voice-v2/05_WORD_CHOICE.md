# 05 — Word Choice for Promos

Vocabulary that makes a spoken promo land. Specificity still beats everything; the persuasion levers carry over, but honestly — no manipulation.

**Role in the app:** word-level choices across the hook, turn, proof, and CTA.

---

## Specificity — the #1 lever

Vague is forgettable; specific is believed.

- ✅ "paid eleven days faster"  ❌ "paid faster"
- ✅ "three hours back a month"  ❌ "saves time"
- ✅ "set up in two minutes"  ❌ "quick and easy"

**Always the founder's real numbers. Never invented.**

---

## Action verbs — what it does, plainly

"send," "get paid," "chase," "track," "build," "ship." Lead lines with the verb where you can: "Get paid before you've closed the laptop."

---

## Absolutes — only when true

"never," "always," "every" grab attention — but only use them if they're defensible. "You'll never send an awkward follow-up again" works *if the product actually does that*. Otherwise, drop it.

---

## Negative → positive framing

Naming the problem first grabs attention; the fix lands the relief. "Stop chasing payments — start getting paid on time." Open on the pain, close on the outcome.

---

## Time language

Ground it in time: "today," "this week," "in two minutes," "same day." Time makes the benefit concrete and immediate.

---

## Honest persuasion (not manipulation)

Use real, earned triggers:

| Use | Example |
|---|---|
| Real social proof | "Freelancers are getting paid the same day." |
| Genuine specificity | "Eleven days faster, on average." |
| True cost-of-inaction | "Every late invoice is hours you spend chasing." |

**Do not use** fabricated FOMO, fake scarcity, invented stats, or fear-mongering. If it isn't true for this product, it doesn't go in the promo.
