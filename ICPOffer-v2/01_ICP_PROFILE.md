# 01 — ICP Profile

The framework the app uses to define a user's ideal customer from their input. This is the anchor of the whole brain — every pain, desire, objection, offer, and promo script targets the person defined here.

**App users:** solo founders · vibe coders · indie hackers (the framework assumes input comes from one of them and must work from as little as a paragraph).
**What it defines:** the *user's* customer — i.e. who the user is selling to.

---

## Role in the app

- **Stage 2 (Build Brain):** the app builds the ICP from the user's product description.
- **Feeds everything downstream:** `02_PAIN_POINTS`, `03_DESIRES`, and `04_OBJECTIONS` all hang off this person; Stage 3 promo scripts all speak to them.
- **If input is too thin:** ask one question — "Who's paying you today, or who do you most want to?" — then build.

---

## The ICP Schema (what the app produces)

| Field | What it is | Required |
|---|---|---|
| **Core identity** | Who they are, in one line | Yes |
| **Situation / trigger** | What's going on that makes them need this *now* | Yes |
| **Sub-segments** | 2–4 concrete customer types within the ICP | Yes |
| **Awareness stage** | How aware they are of the problem/solution (drives the script angle) | Yes |
| **Beliefs** | What they already believe about the problem | Yes |
| **Fears** | What they're afraid of | Yes |
| **Self-image** | How they see themselves (never insult it) | Yes |
| **Their language** | Words they actually use — mined for hooks + voiceover | Yes |

---

## Method (how the app derives it)

1. From the product description, infer **who has the pain** the product solves.
2. **Segment** into 2–4 concrete types (don't stay generic — "freelancers" → "designers, writers, devs working solo").
3. Pick the **primary sub-segment** to target first (the one with the sharpest pain + ability to pay).
4. Profile **psychographics** (beliefs / fears / self-image).
5. Capture **their language** verbatim — this feeds the hook and the voiceover.

---

## Awareness Stages (drives which promo angle to use)

Reframed for video openings — where the viewer is determines how the promo must start.

| Stage | Who | Emotion | Best video opening |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Unaware** | Doesn't know they have the problem | Content, distracted | Pattern interrupt — name a hidden cost |
| **Problem Aware** | Feels the pain, no name for it | Frustrated | Call out the exact pain in their words |
| **Solution Aware** | Knows solutions exist, not yours | Skeptical, comparing | "Most tools do X — here's what's different" |
| **Product Aware** | Knows you, not convinced | Cautious | Proof + risk reversal up front |
| **Most Aware** | Ready, needs a nudge | Motivated | Straight to the offer + CTA |

## Worked Example (neutral) — InvoiceZen

- **Core identity:** Solo freelancers who are great at their craft but hate the money-admin side of running a business.
- **Situation / trigger:** Just took on more clients than they can invoice cleanly; a late payment just stung.
- **Sub-segments:** (1) Freelance designers, (2) freelance writers, (3) solo developers, (4) consultants billing hourly.
- **Awareness stage:** Mostly Problem Aware — they feel the admin pain but blame themselves, not their tools.
- **Beliefs:** "Invoicing is just annoying busywork I have to eat." "Chasing payments is unprofessional but unavoidable."
- **Fears:** Looking amateur to clients; losing money to forgotten/late invoices; spending evenings on admin instead of work.
- **Self-image:** A skilled professional, not a "business person" — they want tools that make them *look* professional without becoming accountants.
- **Their language:** "chasing invoices," "getting paid late," "spreadsheet hell," "awkward follow-up email," "billable hours."

---

## Output note (video / voiceover)

Every promo speaks to **one sub-segment at one awareness stage** — never all of them at once. Use the ICP's own language verbatim in the first line; keep spoken sentences short so the voiceover breathes.
