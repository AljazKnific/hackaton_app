# 01 — CTA Philosophy

The most important reframe in this folder: **a promo is not a tweet.** On an organic feed, no CTA often wins. In a 30-second promo, the CTA is the whole point — you end on one clear ask, every time.

**Role in the app:** governs the **CTA beat (22–30s)** of every Stage 3 promo script.

---

## The Core Principle

> **Every promo ends on exactly one direct CTA — pointed at the after-state, phrased for the lowest-friction first step.**

A promo that doesn't ask, wastes the attention it just earned. The viewer watched 30 seconds; tell them precisely what to do next.

---

## Why this inverts the old rule

The source data ("no CTA beats hard CTA") came from organic tweets, where an ask interrupts a feed and kills reach. A promo is a different format with a different job: it's a pitch the viewer chose to watch. The equivalent of "letting content do the selling" here is **making the CTA feel like the obvious next step**, not omitting it.

---

## The Three Rules

1. **One ask.** Never stack CTAs. Pick the single next step.
2. **Lowest friction.** "Send your first invoice" beats "sign up for a plan."
3. **Point at the outcome.** The CTA names the after-state ("get paid faster"), not the feature ("try our dashboard").

---

## Soft vs. Direct

Both still exist — but "soft" here means *how hard the push is*, not *whether there's an ask*:

- **Direct** (most-aware viewer): "Start free — your first invoice takes two minutes."
- **Soft** (less-aware viewer): "See how fast you could get paid." — still a clear next step, lower commitment.

Match the intensity to awareness (see `05`), but there is always an ask.
