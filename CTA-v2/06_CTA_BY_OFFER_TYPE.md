# 06 — CTA by Offer Type

The right ask depends on what the founder is actually selling and how someone buys it.

**Role in the app:** the app picks the CTA family from the offer defined in `ICPOffer-v2/05_OFFER_POSITIONING`.

---

## Self-Serve App (free / freemium / trial)
- **Goal:** get them into the product now.
- **CTA:** Direct-Action. "Start free — first invoice in two minutes."
- **First step:** the smallest in-product action, not "choose a plan."

---

## Paid Product (one-time or subscription)
- **Goal:** a confident purchase decision.
- **CTA:** Direct with risk-removal. "Try it free for 14 days — cancel anytime."
- **Lead with:** the guarantee or trial to lower the stakes.

---

## Service / Done-For-You (higher ticket)
- **Goal:** start a conversation, not a checkout.
- **CTA:** Contact/Qualify. "Book a 15-minute call to see if it fits."
- **First step:** a low-pressure conversation, lightly qualified.

---

## Pre-Launch / Waitlist
- **Goal:** capture intent before launch.
- **CTA:** Join. "Get early access — join the list."
- **Only real scarcity:** never fabricate a countdown or "spots left."

---

## Community / Free Resource
- **Goal:** a warm, zero-risk entry.
- **CTA:** Get/Join. "Grab the free template" / "Join the community."
- **Use when:** the product needs nurture before purchase.

---

## Rule

Match the ask to how the thing is actually bought. A self-serve app should never end on "book a call," and a high-ticket service should never end on "start free."
