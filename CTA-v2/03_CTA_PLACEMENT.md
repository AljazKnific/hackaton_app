# 03 — CTA Placement

In a promo there's only one right place for the CTA: **the end.** The question isn't *whether* or *where* — it's how to land it so it feels earned.

**Role in the app:** the CTA occupies the final beat (22–30s) of the 30-second script.

---

## The Beat

| Time | Beat | CTA's job |
|---|---|---|
| 0–22s | Hook → Problem → Turn → Proof | earn the ask |
| **22–30s** | **CTA** | **one clear next step** |

The first 22 seconds are the value; the last 8 convert it. Never open on the ask — a cold CTA in the first 3 seconds has nothing behind it.

---

## Landing it

- **Bridge from the proof.** The CTA should feel like the natural conclusion of what was just shown ("...so stop chasing payments — start here").
- **Restate the outcome, then the action.** Remind them of the after-state, then tell them the step.
- **End on the action, not a fade.** The last spoken line is the ask. Don't trail off after it.

---

## One placement rule

> Value first (0–22s), CTA last (22–30s), always exactly once.
