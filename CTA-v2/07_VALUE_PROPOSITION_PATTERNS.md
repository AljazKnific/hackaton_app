# 07 — Value Framing in the CTA

How to frame the value so the ask feels worth it. These sharpen the words around the CTA.

**Role in the app:** applied to the proof (15–22s) and CTA (22–30s) beats.

---

## Show, don't announce
- Don't say "free" as a headline — it can read cheap. Just state the low-risk offer plainly: "Free until your first invoice gets paid."
- Let the specificity carry the value.

---

## Specific numbers beat adjectives
- "Get paid 11 days faster" beats "get paid faster."
- "Two minutes to set up" beats "quick and easy."
- Always the user's real numbers — never invented.

---

## Simple, but proven
- Viewers want simple solutions but distrust "easy." Pair the simplicity with a concrete proof point: "Setup takes two minutes — freelancers are getting paid the same day."

---

## Outcome language, not feature language

| Feature framing (weak) | Outcome framing (strong) |
|---|---|
| "Automated reminders" | "Never send an awkward follow-up again" |
| "Invoice templates" | "Look professional in one tap" |
| "Payment dashboard" | "Know exactly who owes you, at a glance" |

---

## Rule

The CTA sells the after-state. Frame value as something the viewer *feels*, backed by one real number.
