# 02 — Persuasion Levers

The honest persuasion principles behind the triggers — *why* they move people. (The palette is `01`; how persuasion is structured across the script arc is `WritingSystem-v2/04`.)

**Role in the app:** informs which levers a promo pulls, and how to pull them honestly.

---

## The Levers (honest Cialdini)

### Authority
- **Use:** show real proof — results, experience, how it works. Never just claim it.
- **Promo example:** "Freelancers get paid eleven days faster on average."

### Social Proof
- **Use:** real others, real behavior.
- **Promo example:** "Thousands of freelancers stopped chasing payments."

### Reciprocity
- **Use:** give value inside the promo itself (a genuine insight), don't announce "free."
- **Promo example:** teach the one timing trick that gets invoices paid — then show the product does it.

### Consistency / Identity
- **Use:** connect to who they already are or want to be.
- **Promo example:** "You're a pro. Your invoicing should look like it."

### Liking
- **Use:** human, warm, peer-to-peer tone (not a differentiator on its own — baseline).

### Scarcity — ONLY if real
- **Use:** a genuine limit or deadline, honored. Never manufactured.
- 🚩 If it isn't literally true, don't use it.

---

## The Persuasion Order

1. **Recognition** — make them feel understood first.
2. **Emotion** — the trigger (`01`) captures attention.
3. **Proof** — a real lever (authority/social proof) earns belief.
4. **Belief shift** — replace the old belief with a truer one.
5. **Action** — one clear CTA.

---

## What This Never Does

- No manufactured fear, guilt, or urgency.
- No fabricated stats, reviews, or scarcity.
- No persuasion by claim alone — always show.

If a lever requires bending the truth for *this* product, it doesn't go in the promo.
