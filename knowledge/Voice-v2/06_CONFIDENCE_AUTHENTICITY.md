# 06 — Confidence & Authenticity (Spoken)

How to sound sure without sounding fake — read aloud.

**Role in the app:** tone-level guardrails applied across the script.

---

## Confidence

- **No hedging on what the product does.** "This gets you paid faster," not "this might sort of help you get paid faster."
- **Hedge only genuine variability.** "Most freelancers see it within a week" is honest; "this could possibly maybe work" is weak.
- **Guide, don't bark.** "Here's how it works" lands better than "do this now." Confident ≠ commanding.

---

## Authenticity

- **Plain over polished.** Real, conversational phrasing beats corporate gloss. "Getting paid late is the worst" is more human than "payment latency is a challenge."
- **A touch of honesty builds trust.** A single real, relatable line ("Nobody starts a business to chase invoices") earns the viewer. Use sparingly.
- **No false modesty, no bragging.** State what's true and move on.

---

## Delivery guardrails (voiceover)

- **No exclamation pile-ups.** Energy comes from word choice and pace, not "!!!".
- **Emojis/slang:** irrelevant to voiceover — ignore. (They live in on-screen text, not the read.)
- **One emotional peak per promo.** If every line strains for intensity, none of it lands.

---

## Voice check (before finalizing a script)

1. Does it sound like a real person talking, not an ad?
2. Is every claim specific and true?
3. Is it confident without hype?
4. Is it about the viewer?
5. Can every line be said in one breath?
