# 03 — Voice Selection by Product Type

How the app picks the tone mode based on what the founder is building. This is the "best-fit voice" logic: read the product, choose the tone, then combine with the general foundation (`01`, `10`).

**Role in the app:** runs in Stage 2/3 — maps the user's product to a tone mode.

---

## Selection Map

| What they're building | Default tone (from `01`) | Why |
|---|---|---|
| B2B / SaaS / fintech / dev tools | **Calm Authority** | trust and competence sell here |
| Consumer app / wellness / education / creator tool | **Warm & Friendly** | approachability and relatability win |
| Growth tool / launch / bold consumer brand | **Bold & Energetic** | momentum and excitement fit the category |
| Technical / indie-hacker / infra tool | **Straightforward** | the audience distrusts hype |

---

## How the app decides

1. Read the product description + ICP.
2. Match to the closest row above → pick the tone mode.
3. If it spans two (e.g. a dev tool that's also playful), pick the one that matches the **ICP's** expectation, not the founder's excitement.
4. Combine the chosen mode with the general foundation (`01`) and assemble (`10`).

---

## Adjusting for the founder's own brand

If the founder gives brand cues (playful, premium, blunt, etc.), nudge the tone toward it — but keep it within one of the four modes so delivery stays clean. The product type sets the default; the founder's brand fine-tunes it.

---

## Rule

The tone serves the **viewer**, not the founder's mood. A serious buyer needs Calm Authority even if the founder is hyped; a playful consumer audience needs Warmth even if the founder is reserved.
