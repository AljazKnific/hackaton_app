# 02 — Pain Points

How the app extracts and prioritizes the user's customer pains. Each pain becomes a **promo-video angle** — the hook and problem beats are built from it.

**Feeds:** Stage 3 script generation (the hook + problem beats) and `06_CONTENT_ANGLE_MAP`.

---

## Role in the app

- **Stage 2:** from the ICP, the app derives the customer's top pains.
- **Stage 3:** each pain seeds a promo — the 0–8s hook + problem beats are written straight from a pain.
- **Priority matters:** the app ranks pains so the most resonant one leads the first script.

---

## The Pain Schema (what the app produces per pain)

| Field | What it is |
|---|---|
| **Pain name** | Short label for the problem |
| **How it shows up** | 2–4 concrete symptoms in the customer's real life |
| **Emotional layer** | What it makes them *feel* (the part the voiceover lands on) |
| **Video angle** | The one-line framing the promo opens with |

---

## Method (how the app derives pains)

1. For the primary sub-segment, list the **frictions** the product removes.
2. Translate each friction into a **symptom the customer would recognize** ("spreadsheet hell," not "inefficient workflows").
3. Add the **emotional layer** — what the symptom costs them beyond time/money (pride, stress, self-image).
4. Write the **video angle** in the customer's own words.
5. **Prioritize** (see below) so the sharpest pain leads.

---

## Worked Example (neutral) — InvoiceZen pains

### 1. Getting paid late
- Sends invoices, then waits weeks; cash flow is unpredictable
- Has to send awkward "just following up" emails
- Some invoices slip through the cracks entirely

**Emotional layer:** feels powerless and a little unprofessional.
**Video angle:** "You did the work weeks ago. So why haven't you been paid?"

### 2. Spreadsheet hell
- Tracks invoices in a messy spreadsheet or not at all
- Loses track of who owes what
- Rebuilds the same invoice from scratch every time

**Emotional layer:** feels disorganized and behind.
**Video angle:** "Your invoicing system is a spreadsheet and a prayer."

### 3. Looking amateur
- DIY invoices look thrown-together next to the quality of their actual work
- No consistent branding or numbering
- Clients quietly take them less seriously

**Emotional layer:** fears being seen as less professional than they are.
**Video angle:** "Great work, amateur invoice. Guess which one your client remembers."

### 4. Admin eating billable time
- Hours a month lost to invoicing and follow-ups
- Evenings spent on money-admin instead of real work or rest

**Emotional layer:** resentment — this isn't why they went solo.
**Video angle:** "You went freelance for freedom. Now you're an unpaid accountant."

---

## Pain Priority (which pain leads the promo)

The app ranks by resonance + purchase intent:

| Priority | Pain | Why it leads |
|---|---|---|
| 1 | Getting paid late | Highest stakes — it's their money, right now |
| 2 | Admin eating billable time | Universal, quantifiable ("hours a month") |
| 3 | Spreadsheet hell | Instantly recognizable symptom |
| 4 | Looking amateur | Emotional, identity-level |

**Rule for any product:** lead with the pain that costs the customer the most *money or time*, since that's the easiest to dramatize and quantify in a 30-sec promo.

---

## Output note (video / voiceover)

The hook (0–3s) states the pain; the problem beat (3–8s) twists the knife with a symptom. Keep it in the customer's language, spoken in short lines. Never open a promo with the product — open with their pain.
