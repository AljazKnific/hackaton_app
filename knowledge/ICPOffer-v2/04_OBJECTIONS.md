# 04 — Objections & Reframes

How the app surfaces the reasons a customer *won't* buy — and turns each into a reframe the promo can pre-handle. A promo that answers the objection before it's raised converts far better.

**Feeds:** Stage 3 scripts (objection-handling promos) and the CTA beat (remove the last hesitation before the ask).

---

## Role in the app

- **Stage 2:** from the ICP + offer, the app lists the top objections the customer will have.
- **Stage 3:** each objection can become its own promo, or fold into the proof/CTA beats of a main promo.

---

## The Objection Schema (what the app produces per objection)

| Field | What it is |
|---|---|
| **Objection** | What the customer says (their words) |
| **What they mean** | The surface concern |
| **Deeper fear** | The real hesitation underneath |
| **Reframe** | The spoken response that dissolves it |
| **Video angle** | How a promo raises + answers it |

---

## Method (how the app derives objections)

1. For the offer, list the **top 3–5 reasons this customer hesitates** (price, effort, trust, timing, "I already have a way").
2. For each, separate **what they say** from the **deeper fear** — the reframe must speak to the fear.
3. Write the **reframe** as one or two spoken lines, not a paragraph.
4. Turn it into a **video angle** — a promo that names the doubt out loud earns trust fast.

---

## Worked Example (neutral) — InvoiceZen objections

### 1. "I already use spreadsheets / PayPal."
- **What they mean:** I have a way that's free.
- **Deeper fear:** Switching is a hassle for marginal gain.
- **Reframe:** "Spreadsheets don't chase late payments. This does — automatically. That's the difference between free and getting paid."
- **Video angle:** "Your free invoicing setup is costing you money. Here's how."

### 2. "It's too expensive for a side income."
- **What they mean:** Budget concern.
- **Deeper fear:** Not worth it at their income level.
- **Reframe:** "It's less than a coffee a week, and it gets you paid eleven days sooner. One recovered invoice pays for a year."
- **Video angle:** "The math on whether invoicing software is 'worth it' for freelancers."

### 3. "Switching my invoicing is a pain."
- **What they mean:** Migration friction.
- **Deeper fear:** Wasting an afternoon setting it up.
- **Reframe:** "Your first invoice is out in under two minutes. There's nothing to migrate — you just start."
- **Video angle:** "Set up your invoicing in the time it takes to make coffee."

### 4. "My clients are fine with how I invoice now."
- **What they mean:** No visible problem.
- **Deeper fear:** Fixing something that isn't broken.
- **Reframe:** "They're fine — but 'fine' still pays you late. Professional invoices with auto-reminders get you paid faster, and clients respect it more."
- **Video angle:** "'My clients don't mind' — and other reasons freelancers get paid late."

---

## How to use objections in promos

| Objection type | Promo move |
|---|---|
| "I already have a way" | Contrast promo — show the hidden cost of the free/manual way |
| Price | ROI-math promo — cost of the tool vs. cost of the problem |
| Effort / switching | Speed promo — "set up in two minutes" |
| "Not broken" | Cost-of-inaction promo — reframe "fine" as "quietly losing" |

---

## Output note (video / voiceover)

An objection promo names the doubt in the hook ("You think X — here's the problem with that"), then reframes in the turn. Say the reframe plainly and confidently; hedging kills it. One objection per promo — don't stack them.
