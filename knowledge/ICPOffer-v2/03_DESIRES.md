# 03 — Desires & Dreamland

How the app defines what the user's customer actually *wants*. Desires power the **after-state** in the offer and the payoff beat of every promo.

**Feeds:** `05_OFFER_POSITIONING` (after-state) and Stage 3 scripts (the turn + CTA beats).

---

## Role in the app

- **Stage 2:** from the ICP + pains, the app derives the customer's desires (every pain has a mirror-image desire).
- **Stage 3:** the desire is what the promo *promises* — it's the emotional destination the CTA points to.

---

## The Desire Schema (what the app produces per desire)

| Field | What it is |
|---|---|
| **Desire name** | The outcome they want |
| **Functional win** | The concrete, practical result |
| **Emotional win** | How it makes them feel (confidence, pride, relief, control) |
| **Dreamland line** | A word-for-word "after" sentence usable in a script |

---

## Method (how the app derives desires)

1. **Mirror each top pain** — the desire is usually its inverse ("paid late" → "paid on time, automatically").
2. Separate the **functional win** (what happens) from the **emotional win** (how it feels) — voiceover lands on the emotional one.
3. Write a **dreamland line** in the customer's voice that a promo can use verbatim.
4. Keep desires **specific and believable** — overpromising kills a 30-sec promo's credibility.

---

## Worked Example (neutral) — InvoiceZen desires

### 1. Get paid on time, without asking
- **Functional win:** invoices go out instantly, reminders chase automatically
- **Emotional win:** relief + control — no more awkward follow-ups
- **Dreamland line:** "Money lands in your account while you're doing the work you actually enjoy."

### 2. Look as professional as their work
- **Functional win:** clean, branded invoices every time
- **Emotional win:** pride — clients take them seriously
- **Dreamland line:** "Your invoice looks as sharp as the work you just delivered."

### 3. Get admin off their plate
- **Functional win:** hours a month back, no spreadsheet
- **Emotional win:** freedom — back to why they went solo
- **Dreamland line:** "Spend zero evenings on money-admin ever again."

### 4. Feel in control of their cash flow
- **Functional win:** one clear view of what's paid, pending, overdue
- **Emotional win:** confidence — no nasty surprises
- **Dreamland line:** "Always know exactly who owes you what, at a glance."

---

## Dreamland Bank (concrete "after" lines)

Reusable paint-strokes the promo can drop in verbatim:

- "Get paid faster, without ever sending an awkward reminder again."
- "Every invoice out in under a minute."
- "Look professional the second your client opens the email."
- "Your evenings back. Your cash flow clear."

---

## ICP Language for Desire (use in hooks + CTAs)

**Power words the customer responds to:**
paid faster · automatic · effortless · professional · clear · in control · done in seconds · no more chasing · off your plate · peace of mind

---

## Output note (video / voiceover)

The desire is the promo's payoff — it belongs in the **turn** (8–15s, "here's what life looks like instead") and the **CTA** (22–30s). Say it as a felt outcome, not a feature. Keep the dreamland line short enough to land in one breath.
