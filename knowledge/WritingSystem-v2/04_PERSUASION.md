# 04 — Persuasion

Activate recognition, trust, and belief change using honest psychological triggers and emotional pacing. Persuasion here means *replacing an old belief with a truer one* — never manipulation.

**Role in the app:** shapes the emotional arc across all five beats.

---

## Core Principles

1. **Recognition** — make the viewer feel understood before teaching.
2. **Belief shift** — swap an old belief for a new one; don't just add facts.
3. **Proof through experience** — real results and specifics earn trust.
4. **Curiosity first** — open the loop, then deliver once attention is earned.
5. **Emotion before logic** — feeling captures, reasoning confirms.

---

## Honest Triggers

| Trigger | How to use |
|---|---|
| Identity | speak to who they are ("freelancers who'd rather create than chase") |
| Curiosity | open a loop worth resolving |
| Authority | show real proof/experience, not claims |
| Contrast | old belief vs. new belief = productive tension |
| Relatability | mirror their exact frustration |
| Hope | make the better version feel reachable |

---

## Frameworks (honest use only)

- **Cialdini (subset):** reciprocity (give value in the promo itself), authority (show proof), social proof (real users only), liking (human tone). Use scarcity *only* if it's real.
- **Schwartz awareness:** match the message to the viewer's awareness stage (`ICPOffer-v2/01`) — unaware needs the hidden cost; most-aware needs the offer.
- **Hormozi:** make the insight so useful it could be sold — then give it away in the promo.

---

## Emotional Arc (mapped to the beats)

1. **Tension** (Hook/Problem) — name the pain or confusion.
2. **Curiosity** (into the Turn) — a question or contrast.
3. **Realization** (Turn) — the reframe or fix.
4. **Proof** (Proof beat) — the real number or result.
5. **Resolution** (CTA) — clarity + one confident next step.

---

## Do / Don't

**Do:** lead with pain or curiosity · create contrast before the insight · show real proof · let the viewer reach the conclusion.

**Don't:** use fear, guilt, or fabricated scarcity · preach morality · persuade by authority alone (show, don't just claim) · invent numbers or results.
