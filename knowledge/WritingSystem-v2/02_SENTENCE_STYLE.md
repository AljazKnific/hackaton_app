# 02 — Sentence Style

Make every line confident, effortless, and human — short enough to digest, sharp enough to remember. Written for the ear.

**Role in the app:** line-level construction across the whole script.

---

## Clarity Over Cleverness
- Every line understood on first listen.
- If it needs a re-read, it's too complex — simplify.

## Plain Language
- Cut jargon and filler. Say the plain word.
- Avoid: leverage, utilize, synergy, ecosystem, paradigm, "game-changing."
- Use: use, tool, system, get, change.
- Exception: a niche term is fine if the founder's ICP genuinely uses it.

## Tight Syntax
- Every word earns its place. Cut "I think," "really," "very," "just," "actually."
- **Before:** "I think what I'm really trying to say is that getting paid on time is quite important."
- **After:** "Getting paid on time changes everything."

## Contrast Within Lines
- Pair opposing ideas for tension: "not a nicer invoice — a faster payment," "less chasing, more building."

## Short, Spoken Lines
- 5–12 words per line, one breath each (delivery rules: `Voice-v2/07`).
- Alternate short and slightly longer lines for rhythm.

## Openers That Work
- "The truth is…" · "Most freelancers…" · "You don't need…" · "Here's what changed…"
- Avoid opening on filler ("so," "basically," "honestly") — except a rare, genuine "honestly" for a human beat.

---

## Rule

Read every line aloud. If it sounds robotic, stiff, or over-clever, rewrite it until it sounds like a sharp person talking.
