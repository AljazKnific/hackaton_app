# 02 — CTA Types

The kinds of asks a promo can close on. The app picks one based on the founder's conversion goal and the viewer's awareness.

**Role in the app:** supplies the CTA options for the 22–30s beat.

---

## Direct-Action CTAs (most common)

The viewer does the thing right now. Best for low-friction, self-serve products.

- **Start / Try:** "Start free — your first invoice takes two minutes."
- **Get:** "Get your first invoice out today."

**Best for:** free trials, freemium apps, anything with instant self-serve.

---

## Soft-Commitment CTAs

A smaller step for a colder viewer — still a clear action, lower stakes.

- **See / Watch:** "See how fast you could get paid."
- **Check:** "Check what your invoices could look like."

**Best for:** unaware/problem-aware viewers, higher-consideration products.

---

## Contact / Qualify CTAs

The next step is a conversation. Best for services or higher-ticket offers.

- **Book:** "Book a 15-minute setup call."
- **Message:** "Message us 'invoices' and we'll set you up."

**Best for:** services, agencies, high-ticket or done-for-you offers.

---

## Waitlist / Early-Access CTAs

For pre-launch or capacity-limited products.

- **Join:** "Join the early-access list."

**Best for:** pre-launch, beta, genuine capacity limits (never fake scarcity).

---

## Picking one

| Product type | Default CTA |
|---|---|
| Self-serve app / freemium | Direct-Action (Start/Try) |
| Higher-consideration tool | Soft-Commitment (See/Watch) |
| Service / high-ticket | Contact/Qualify (Book/Message) |
| Pre-launch | Waitlist (Join) |

Whatever the type: **one ask, lowest friction, names the outcome.**
