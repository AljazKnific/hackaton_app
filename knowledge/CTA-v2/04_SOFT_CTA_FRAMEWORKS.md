# 04 — CTA Phrasing Frameworks

Reusable ways to phrase the closing ask so it converts. Each is a fill-in template.

**Role in the app:** the app writes the CTA line from one of these, using the user's product + after-state.

---

## Outcome → Action
- **Pattern:** `[restate the after-state] — [the one step].`
- **Example:** "Stop chasing payments — start free today."
- **Best for:** most promos; the default.

---

## Time-to-Value
- **Pattern:** `[Result] in [short, true timeframe] — [step].`
- **Example:** "Get your first invoice out in two minutes — start now."
- **Best for:** fast, self-serve products.

---

## Remove-the-Risk
- **Pattern:** `[Low/zero-risk framing]. [Step].`
- **Example:** "Free until your first invoice gets paid. Try it."
- **Best for:** overcoming hesitation; strong for cold viewers.

---

## Direct Address
- **Pattern:** `If you're [their situation], [step].`
- **Example:** "If you're tired of getting paid late, start here."
- **Best for:** mirror-hook promos; warm viewers.

---

## Same-Step Simplicity
- **Pattern:** `[The literally-first action], nothing else.`
- **Example:** "Just send one invoice. That's the whole setup."
- **Best for:** products where the first action IS the aha moment.

---

## Rule for all

Specific action + named outcome + one step. No "learn more," no stacking, no hedging.
