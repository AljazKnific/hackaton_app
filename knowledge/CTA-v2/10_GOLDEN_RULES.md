# 10 — Golden Rules & Never Do

The non-negotiables for promo CTAs. (These invert the old Twitter-feed rules — a promo is a pitch, not an organic post.)

---

## Golden Rules

1. **Every promo ends on one CTA.** No ask = wasted attention.
2. **One ask only.** Never stack CTAs.
3. **Lowest-friction step wins.** Point at the smallest first action.
4. **CTA names the outcome**, not the feature.
5. **Match intensity to awareness** — soft for cold viewers, direct for warm (see `05`).
6. **Value first (0–22s), CTA last (22–30s).**
7. **Say it plainly and confidently** — no hedging, no "you might want to."

---

## Never Do

| Never | Why |
|---|---|
| End with no CTA | The viewer watched — tell them what's next |
| Stack multiple CTAs | Splits focus, lowers action |
| Open on the CTA | Nothing earned it yet |
| Generic "learn more" / "click here" | No reason to act, no outcome named |
| Feature-led ask ("try our dashboard") | Sell the result, not the tool |
| Fake urgency ("only 24 hours!") that isn't real | Destroys trust; the app must never fabricate it |
| Hedged asks ("maybe check it out") | Weak; kills conversion |

---

## The One-Sentence Summary

> Earn 22 seconds of attention, then spend the last 8 pointing at one clear, low-friction step toward the outcome.
