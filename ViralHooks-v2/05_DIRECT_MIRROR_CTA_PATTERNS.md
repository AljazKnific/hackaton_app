# 05 — Direct, Mirror & CTA Hooks

Speak straight at the viewer — name their exact situation, their fear, their cost — then point them to the one action.

**Best for:** product-aware and most-aware viewers; the CTA beat (22–30s).
**Video beat:** these can open a promo (mirror) or close it (CTA).
**Rule:** the CTA is required in a promo — a 30-sec video is a pitch, so it ends on ONE clear ask (this is the opposite of the old organic-tweet "no CTA" rule).

---

## Hook A — The Mirror

Name the viewer's exact situation so precisely they feel spoken to directly.

**Neutral example (InvoiceZen):**
> "You finished the work three weeks ago. You still haven't been paid. And emailing again feels awkward."

**Formula:** `[their exact situation] / [the second detail that confirms you get it] / [the feeling they haven't said out loud]`

**Critical elements:** use the ICP's real situation and words; validate the feeling; the more precise, the more it lands as "that's me."

---

## Hook B — The Direct Stakes

Open in second person with what the problem is costing them right now.

**Neutral example:**
> "Late invoices are costing you more than money — they're costing you the hours you spend chasing them."

**Formula:** `[the problem] is costing you [more than the obvious cost] — [the hidden cost they feel]`

**Critical elements:** speak directly ("you"); name a real, felt cost; make it present-tense and immediate.

---

## Hook C — The Underdog Proof

Prove it's achievable by showing an unlikely person who pulled it off. (Removes the "that won't work for me" barrier.)

**Neutral example:**
> "A freelancer who 'hates admin' set this up in two minutes and got paid the next day."

**Formula:** `[relatable/unlikely person] [did the thing] in [low effort] and [got the result]`

**Critical elements:** the person mirrors the viewer's self-doubt; effort is low and believable; result is real.

---

## Hook D — The Real-Deadline Offer (CTA)

A time-bound offer — but only a genuine one.

**Neutral example:**
> "Free until your first invoice gets paid — start in the next two minutes."

**Formula:** `[genuine offer / real limit] / [low-effort first step] / [one clear action]`

**Critical elements:** one action only; the first step is tiny; the ask is direct and specific.

> ⚠️ **Never fake urgency.** Only use a deadline or scarcity claim if it's real and will be honored. Fabricated "deleting soon" / "only today" urgency erodes trust and misrepresents the product — the app must not generate it.

---

## CTA Rules (every promo ends on one)

- **One ask.** Never stack CTAs — pick the single next step.
- **Make it tiny.** "Send your first invoice" beats "sign up for a plan."
- **Point at the after-state**, not the feature ("get paid faster," not "try our dashboard").
- **Say it plainly.** No hedging, no "you might want to." Confident and direct.
