# 04 — Reframe & Myth-Buster Hooks

Challenge accepted wisdom, redefine the problem, or reveal the variable everyone misses. These make the viewer rethink something they took for granted.

**Best for:** solution-aware viewers comparing options, "I already have a way" objections.
**Video beat:** open on the Hook, deliver the reframe in the Turn (8–15s).
**Rule:** reframe honestly. Don't invent villains or call real tools "scams" — expose the real hidden cost, not a fake one.

---

## Hook A — The Misdirection

Set up an expectation, then defuse it into something simpler and truer.

**Neutral example (InvoiceZen):**
> "Getting paid on time has nothing to do with your clients. It's about *when* the invoice hits their inbox."

**Formula:** `[what they'd assume the problem is] / not that / [the real, more mundane cause]`

**Critical elements:** the first line points at the obvious culprit; the second redirects; the reveal is simple and actionable.

---

## Hook B — The Myth-Buster

Name a common piece of advice and show why it quietly costs them.

**Neutral example:**
> "'Just send a polite follow-up' is why freelancers wait a month to get paid. Here's what actually works."

**Formula:** `"[common advice]" is why [the bad outcome persists] / [the better approach]`

**Critical elements:** reference advice people actually follow; show the real cost (not a fabricated one); set up your approach as the fix.

---

## Hook C — The Value Inversion

Contrast a cheap/obvious option against a more valuable one the viewer overlooks.

**Neutral example:**
> "A prettier invoice template won't get you paid. A reminder that sends itself will."

**Formula:** `[obvious cheap fix] [doesn't solve it] / [the overlooked thing] [that actually does]`

**Critical elements:** destroy the obvious approach first; make the better option feel like insider clarity, not upsell.

---

## Hook D — The Redefinition

Take something familiar and reframe what it's really for.

**Neutral example:**
> "Your invoice isn't paperwork. It's the last impression you leave on a client — and the reason they rehire you."

**Formula:** `[familiar thing] isn't [assumed purpose] / it's [surprising truer purpose]`

**Critical elements:** the redefinition must be genuinely true; it should make the viewer see the thing differently for good.

---

## Hook E — The Hidden Variable

Eliminate the obvious explanations, then reveal the one that actually matters.

**Neutral example:**
> "Why do some freelancers get paid in a day and others wait a month? It's not the client. It's not the rate. It's the timing of the invoice."

**Formula:** `Why do [some succeed while others don't]? / It's not [obvious 1]. Not [obvious 2]. / It's [the real, specific variable]`

**Critical elements:** knock down exactly two or three obvious answers; the reveal is specific and real; the script explains the how.

---

## Hook F — The Pattern Thesis

Point out a real pattern the viewer will recognize and want to weigh in on.

**Neutral example:**
> "The freelancers who never chase payments all have one boring habit in common."

**Formula:** `[the group that gets the good outcome] all share [one specific, unglamorous trait]`

**Critical elements:** the pattern is real and recognizable; the trait is specific; it invites the viewer to see themselves in it.
