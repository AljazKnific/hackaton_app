# 07 — Script & Voice Mechanics

How a generated promo should *sound* once a hook is chosen — the writing and delivery rules that turn a hook into a 30-second spoken script.

**Role in the app:** applied after the hook, across the whole script, and again on the voiceover pass. Where `01`–`05` give the psychology, this file makes it sound natural when spoken.

---

## Core Voice

- Sound like a **peer who solved the problem**, not a brand reading a brochure.
- Confident and clear — state things plainly, no corporate hedging.
- Warm and direct, not hype-y or aggressive. Trust comes from specificity, not volume.
- Every claim is backed by a real detail; nothing vague, nothing inflated.

---

## The 3 Core Moves (compressed)

1. **Counterintuitive reframe** — `[what they assume] → [the truer opposite]`, in the first spoken line.
2. **Cost made concrete** — turn a vague frustration into a felt, specific cost (time, money, credibility).
3. **Honest redefinition** — take something familiar and show what it's really for.

---

## Awareness-Stage Targeting

Write for *where the viewer is*, not just the topic (mirrors `ICPOffer-v2/01_ICP_PROFILE`):

| Stage | Move | Example flavor (InvoiceZen) |
|---|---|---|
| **Unaware** | surface the hidden cost | "You're losing hours a month and you've stopped noticing." |
| **Problem Aware** | name the problem clearly | "Getting paid late isn't a client problem — it's a timing problem." |
| **Solution Aware** | show why other fixes fall short | "A nicer template won't chase the payment. This does." |
| **Product Aware** | prove your way works | "Freelancers using it get paid days sooner — here's the proof." |
| **Most Aware** | go straight to the offer + CTA | "Free until your first invoice gets paid. Start now." |

---

## Story Mechanics (for spoken promos)

1. **Character setup** — one concrete detail (who, where, how long).
2. **Unexpected turn** — the simple thing outperforms the obvious thing.
3. **A real line of dialogue** — puts a scene in the viewer's head.
4. **Held payoff** — don't fully resolve until the turn/CTA.
5. **Transformation arc** — before → after, with real numbers.

---

## Writing Rules

**Always:** real, specific numbers · short spoken sentences · one idea per breath · concrete before/after · plain words · a single clear CTA · lead with the pain or the outcome.

**Never:** vague claims ("a lot," "many") · corporate buzzwords (synergy, leverage, paradigm) · fabricated metrics or fake urgency · walls of text · ALL-CAPS or punctuation tricks the ear can't hear · stacked CTAs · hedging ("you might want to") · opening on the product instead of the viewer.

**Spoken cadence:** short sentences for impact, slightly longer to explain. Contractions always. Say numbers as words ("eleven days"). Read every line aloud mentally — if it can't be said in one breath, cut it.

---

## What This System Never Does

- Fabricate results, reviews, scarcity, or specificity.
- Name real competitors as "scams" or use dishonest framing.
- Manufacture fake urgency or pressure.
- Produce generic copy any brand could have written — every promo is built from *this* user's real ICP, pains, and proof.
