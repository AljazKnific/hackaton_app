# 01 — Hook Architecture

> The hook is the first 3 seconds of the promo — the spoken line that stops the scroll. Its job isn't to describe the product; it's to make skipping the video feel like missing out.

**Role in the app:** feeds the **Hook beat (0–3s)** of every Stage 3 promo script. Pulls the pain from `ICPOffer-v2/02_PAIN_POINTS` and the audience from `01_ICP_PROFILE`.
**Output:** a spoken opening line — built to be *heard*, not read.

---

## The Neural Pathway (every strong hook follows this)

1. **Pattern interrupt** — violate the expected opening in the first few words
2. **Self-recognition** — the viewer thinks "that's me / that's my problem"
3. **Curiosity gap** — open a loop the rest of the video closes
4. **Stakes** — make the cost of ignoring it feel real

## The Meta-Pattern

> **[What they assume] → [The turn that contradicts it] → [Specific proof] → [Open loop the promo resolves]**

---

## The Specificity Cascade

Generic is skippable. Specific feels true.

- Not "faster" → **"11 days faster"**
- Not "saves time" → **"3 hours back every month"**
- Not "cheap" → **"less than a coffee a week"**
- Not "a freelancer" → **"a freelance designer juggling five clients"**

**Deployment:** put one concrete number or detail in the first spoken line. Specificity is what makes a viewer believe the promise is real. **Only ever use the founder's real numbers — never invent metrics** (see `02` and `06`).

---

## Universal Hook Requirements (every generated hook must include)

1. **Expectation violation** — contradict the viewer's assumption in the first spoken line
2. **Real specificity** — a concrete number, detail, or moment (from the user's input)
3. **Self-recognition** — the viewer sees their own situation
4. **Miniature story or tension** — setup → turn, with the payoff held back
5. **Open loop** — leave one thing unresolved so they keep watching

---

## The Ultimate Rule

Every hook must make the viewer:

1. **Stop scrolling** (pattern interrupt)
2. **See themselves** ("that's my problem")
3. **Feel the stakes** ("this is costing me")
4. **Need the rest** (open loop)

If a hook doesn't do all four — rewrite it.

**When the hook targets a pain, it must be one the ICP widely and genuinely feels** — not a niche edge case.

---

## Voiceover note

The hook is spoken aloud, so:

- **No ALL-CAPS or punctuation tricks** — the ear can't hear them. Create emphasis with word choice and sentence length instead.
- **Front-load the point** — the first 3–4 spoken words carry the interrupt.
- **One breath, one idea.** If the opening line can't be said in a single breath, it's too long for a hook.
- **Say numbers cleanly** — "eleven days," not "11d."
