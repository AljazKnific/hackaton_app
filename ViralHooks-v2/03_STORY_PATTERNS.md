# 03 — Story Hooks

Open a little movie in the viewer's head, then cut before the payoff. Story hooks work because people can't leave an unfinished story.

**Best for:** relatability, founder/customer origin, transformation proof.
**Video beat:** open on the Hook, let the story carry the Problem (3–8s) and Turn (8–15s).
**Rule:** stories must be true. Use the founder's real customer/origin story — don't fabricate characters.

---

## Hook A — The Humble-Start Transformation

A modest, relatable beginning → a real result → an honest reflection.

**Neutral example (InvoiceZen):**
> "Six months ago I was tracking invoices on a napkin. Last month I got paid faster than I ever have — from the same tiny desk."

**Formula:** `[recent, modest starting point] / [real improvement] in [short timeframe] / [humble detail] / [honest reflection]`

**Critical elements:** modest (not zero) start; recent, believable timeframe; a humble, specific detail; real results, not vanity numbers.

---

## Hook B — The Unexpected Insight

Someone the viewer would underestimate reveals something genuinely smart. Status flip.

**Neutral example:**
> "A freelancer told me the reason she gets paid on time isn't her rates — it's that her invoice lands the second the work is done."

**Formula:** `[unassuming source] / [simple action] / [the insight that reframes the problem]`

**Critical elements:** low expectations up front; the insight is real and usable; end on the reframe, not the person.

---

## Hook C — The Open-Loop Setup

Start a story with a concrete detail and a recent timeframe, then hold the resolution.

**Neutral example:**
> "Last week a freelancer showed me the invoice that finally got her paid in 24 hours."

**Formula:** `[recent timeframe] [specific person/situation] [intriguing detail] — [story continues, not resolved]`

**Critical elements:** concrete, believable setup; recent timeframe; the hook sets up a story the script then pays off; never resolve it in the first line.

---

## Hook D — The Volume + Surprise

A high-volume action produces one surprising moment that reveals the point.

**Neutral example:**
> "We talked to 200 freelancers about getting paid. The same complaint came up every single time — and it wasn't about money."

**Formula:** `[high-volume action + real number] [timeframe] / [one surprising pattern] / [the reveal]`

**Critical elements:** volume real and specific; a genuine "huh" moment; leave the full reveal for the script.

---

## Hook E — The Turning-Point Encounter

A conversation or moment that changed how the founder thinks — teased, not resolved.

**Neutral example:**
> "A client once paid me three weeks late and said 'your invoice got buried.' That one sentence is why this app exists."

**Formula:** `[real encounter] / [the thing said/seen] / [the shift it caused] — [held back until the script]`

**Critical elements:** real and specific; the moment implies a lesson; transformation teased without giving away the how.
